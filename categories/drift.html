BMW in Drifting

While drifting originated in Japan, BMW has carved out its own legendary status in the drift scene. Known for their perfect 50:50 weight distribution, powerful engines, and rear-wheel-drive platforms, BMW vehicles have become increasingly popular among drift enthusiasts. From the iconic M3 series to the powerful M5, these German machines prove that precision engineering and drift culture can create something truly spectacular.

Legendary Drift Cars

BMW M5

The BMW M5, particularly the E60 generation, has become an unexpected hero in the drift scene. Its powerful V10 engine, rear-wheel drive setup, and precise handling characteristics make it a formidable drift machine despite its luxury sedan origins.

Engine S85 V10
Power 500+ HP
Drive RWD
Year 2005-2010

BMW E36 M3

The E36 M3 is a popular choice in the drift community due to its perfect weight distribution, robust chassis, and tunable S50/S52 engine. Its balanced handling and reliable platform make it an excellent drift car for both beginners and professionals.

Engine S50/S52
Power 240+ HP
Drive RWD
Year 1992-1999

BMW E46 M3

The E46 M3 represents the perfect blend of modern technology and raw driving dynamics. Its high-revving S54 engine, sophisticated chassis, and excellent weight distribution make it a favorite among drift enthusiasts seeking precision and power.

Drift Techniques

Clutch Kick

The clutch kick technique involves quickly pressing and releasing the clutch pedal while the car is in gear. This momentarily disrupts power to the wheels, causing them to lose traction and initiate a drift.

Power Over

This technique involves applying enough throttle to break traction at the rear wheels. When entering a corner, the driver applies enough power to cause the rear wheels to spin faster than the speed of the car.

Handbrake Entry

The driver pulls the handbrake while cornering to lock the rear wheels, causing them to lose traction. This is often used to initiate a drift when entering a corner at moderate speeds.

Feint Drift

This technique involves steering quickly in the opposite direction of the turn before steering into the turn. This weight transfer helps initiate the drift by shifting the car's balance and breaking traction.
