The Art of Sliding

Drifting is a driving technique where the driver intentionally oversteers, causing loss of traction in the rear wheels while maintaining control through the entire corner. What began as a racing technique in Japan has evolved into a worldwide motorsport that combines technical skill with artistic expression.

Drift Techniques

Clutch Kick

The clutch kick technique involves quickly pressing and releasing the clutch pedal while the car is in gear. This momentarily disrupts power to the wheels, causing them to lose traction and initiate a drift.

Power Over

This technique involves applying enough throttle to break traction at the rear wheels. When entering a corner, the driver applies enough power to cause the rear wheels to spin faster than the speed of the car.

Handbrake Entry

The driver pulls the handbrake while cornering to lock the rear wheels, causing them to lose traction. This is often used to initiate a drift when entering a corner at moderate speeds.

Feint Drift

This technique involves steering quickly in the opposite direction of the turn before steering into the turn. This weight transfer helps initiate the drift by shifting the car's balance and breaking traction.
